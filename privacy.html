Privacy Policy

Last Updated: June 15, 2023

At Mossleies Care, we are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, disclose, and safeguard your information when you visit our website or use our services. Please read this policy carefully to understand our practices regarding your information and how we will treat it.

1. Information We Collect

We collect various types of information in connection with the services we provide, including personal information that identifies you as an individual, information about how you use our website, and technical data about your device. This includes your name, email address, phone number, billing information, and any other information you voluntarily provide when contacting us, making purchases, or subscribing to our services. We may also collect data about your interaction with our website through cookies and similar technologies.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect primarily to provide, maintain, protect, and improve our current services and to develop new ones. This includes processing transactions, providing customer support, sending service-related announcements, conducting research and analysis to improve our offerings, personalizing your experience, and complying with legal obligations. We may also use your information to communicate with you about promotions, upcoming events, and other news about products and services we offer.

3. Legal Basis for Processing Data

We process your personal data only when we have a lawful basis to do so. This typically includes the necessity to fulfill our contractual obligations to you, to comply with legal requirements, or for our legitimate business interests when they are not overridden by your rights. In some cases, we may process your data based on the consent you have given for specific purposes, which you have the right to withdraw at any time by contacting us through the methods provided in this policy.

4. Data Retention Practices

We retain personal information only for as long as necessary to fulfill the purposes for which it was collected, including for the purposes of satisfying any legal, accounting, or reporting requirements. To determine the appropriate retention period, we consider the amount, nature, and sensitivity of the data, the potential risk of harm from unauthorized use or disclosure, the purposes for which we process your data, and whether we can achieve those purposes through other means. Typically, we retain customer data for seven years following the termination of our relationship unless a longer retention period is required by law.

5. Information Sharing and Disclosure

We do not sell or rent your personal information to third parties. We may share your information with service providers who perform services on our behalf, such as payment processing, data analysis, email delivery, hosting services, customer service, and marketing assistance. These third parties have access to your information only to perform these tasks on our behalf and are obligated not to disclose or use it for any other purpose. We may also disclose your information when required by law or in response to valid requests by public authorities.

6. Cookies and Tracking Technologies

We use cookies and similar tracking technologies to track activity on our website and hold certain information to improve and analyze our service. Cookies are files with small amounts of data that may include an anonymous unique identifier. You can instruct your browser to refuse all cookies or to indicate when a cookie is being sent, but if you do not accept cookies, you may not be able to use some portions of our service. We use both session cookies (which expire when you close your browser) and persistent cookies (which stay on your device until you delete them).

7. Data Security Measures

We implement appropriate technical and organizational measures to protect the security of your personal information, both online and offline. These include encryption of sensitive data, regular security testing of our systems, access controls to limit who can access user information, and training for our employees on data protection best practices. While we strive to use commercially acceptable means to protect your personal information, no method of transmission over the Internet or electronic storage is 100% secure, and we cannot guarantee absolute security.

8. International Data Transfers

Your personal information may be transferred to and maintained on computers located outside of your state, province, country, or other governmental jurisdiction where the data protection laws may differ from those in your jurisdiction. We ensure that appropriate safeguards are in place to require that your personal information will remain protected in accordance with this Privacy Policy when transferred internationally, including the use of standard contractual clauses where necessary. By providing your information to us, you consent to this transfer, storing, or processing.

9. Children's Privacy

Our services are not intended for children under the age of 16, and we do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 16. If we become aware that we have collected personal information from a child under the age of 16 without parental consent, we will take steps to remove that information from our servers. If you believe we might have any information from or about a child under 16, please contact us immediately using the contact information provided in this policy.

10. Your Data Protection Rights

Depending on your location, you may have certain rights regarding your personal information under applicable data protection laws. These may include the right to access, correct, update, or request deletion of your personal information; the right to object to or restrict processing of your personal information; the right to data portability; and the right to withdraw consent where we are processing your data based on consent. To exercise any of these rights, please contact us using the contact details provided in this policy. We will respond to your request within the time period required by applicable law.

11. Third-Party Websites

Our website may contain links to third-party websites or services that are not owned or controlled by Mossleies Care. We have no control over, and assume no responsibility for, the content, privacy policies, or practices of any third-party websites or services. We strongly advise you to review the privacy policy of every site you visit. This Privacy Policy applies only to information collected by our website. The inclusion of any link does not imply endorsement by Mossleies Care or any association with its operators.

12. Changes to This Privacy Policy

We may update our Privacy Policy from time to time to reflect changes in our practices or for other operational, legal, or regulatory reasons. We will notify you of any changes by posting the new Privacy Policy on this page and updating the "Last Updated" date at the top. You are advised to review this Privacy Policy periodically for any changes. Changes to this Privacy Policy are effective when they are posted on this page, except when they require your explicit consent, in which case we will seek it as required by applicable law.

13. Contact Us

If you have any questions about this Privacy Policy, the practices of our website, or your dealings with our services, please contact us at the following: Mossleies Care, 242 Greene St, New York, NY 10003, USA. You may also reach us by phone at (212) 555-0198 or by email at privacy@mossleiescare.com. For all personal data requests, including access requests, deletion requests, and other rights under applicable data protection laws, please contact our Data Protection Officer at dpo@mossleiescare.com.

14. Payment Information Security

All payment transactions are processed through secure payment processors that comply with the Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard (PCI DSS). We do not store your full credit card details on our servers. When you make a payment, your payment information is transmitted directly to our payment processor using secure encryption. We only retain minimal payment information necessary for refunds or fraud prevention purposes, and all such information is stored in an encrypted format with strict access controls.

15. Marketing Communications

If you opt-in to receive marketing communications from us, we may use your personal information to send you newsletters, special offers, and other promotional materials. You can opt-out of receiving these communications at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions included in these communications or by contacting us directly. Even if you opt-out of receiving marketing communications, we may still send you administrative messages relating to account management, service announcements, and similar important service-related communications that are not promotional in nature.

16. Data Protection Officer

Mossleies Care has appointed a Data Protection Officer (DPO) to oversee compliance with data protection laws and this Privacy Policy. The DPO can be contacted at dpo@mossleiescare.com or at our physical address marked for the attention of the Data Protection Officer. The DPO is responsible for monitoring our compliance with applicable data protection laws, providing advice regarding data protection impact assessments, and serving as a point of contact for data subjects and supervisory authorities regarding data protection matters.

17. Data Breach Notification

In the event of a personal data breach that is likely to result in a risk to the rights and freedoms of individuals, we will notify the affected individuals and relevant supervisory authorities as required by applicable law. If we determine that a breach affecting your personal data has occurred, we will notify you without undue delay after becoming aware of the breach, describing the nature of the breach, the likely consequences, and the measures we have taken or propose to take to address the breach. Our breach notification procedure includes assessing the scope and impact of the breach, taking steps to mitigate its effects, and implementing measures to prevent similar breaches in the future.

18. Employee Training and Confidentiality

All Mossleies Care employees who handle personal data are trained in data protection principles and are required to maintain the confidentiality of personal information. Access to personal data is restricted to authorized personnel who have a legitimate business need for the information based on the principle of least privilege. Employees are subject to disciplinary actions, including termination of employment and legal action, for violations of our data protection policies. We conduct regular training sessions to ensure all employees are aware of their data protection obligations.

19. Profiling and Automated Decision Making

We do not engage in automated decision-making or profiling that produces legal effects concerning you or similarly significantly affects you without human intervention. In cases where we might use automated processing that could affect you significantly, we will provide you with meaningful information about the logic involved, as well as the significance and the envisaged consequences of such processing for you. You will have the right to request human intervention, to express your point of view, and to contest the decision made through automated means.

20. Complaints and Grievances

If you have any complaints or concerns about how we handle your personal information, we encourage you to contact us first at privacy@mossleiescare.com so that we can address your concerns directly. If you are not satisfied with our response, you have the right to lodge a complaint with a supervisory authority in your jurisdiction. We take all complaints seriously and will work diligently to resolve any issues you may have regarding our data practices.

21. User Access and Control

You have the right to access the personal information we hold about you and to request corrections if you believe that information is inaccurate or incomplete. You can also request that we delete your personal information, subject to certain exceptions, such as when we need to retain the information for legal compliance or to fulfill our contractual obligations. To exercise these rights, please contact us using the information provided in this policy.

22. Data Minimization

We adhere to the principle of data minimization, which means we only collect and process personal information that is necessary for the purposes for which it is collected. We regularly review our data collection practices to ensure that we are not collecting more information than is needed. If we determine that certain data is no longer necessary, we will securely delete or anonymize it in accordance with our data retention policies.

23. Transparency and Communication

We are committed to being transparent about how we handle your personal information. This Privacy Policy is part of our commitment to communicate clearly and openly with you about our data practices. We will provide you with any updates or changes to this policy in a timely manner, ensuring that you are always informed about how your information is being used and protected.

24. Data Protection Impact Assessments

We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) when initiating new projects or processing activities that may impact the privacy of individuals. DPIAs help us identify and mitigate potential risks to personal data and ensure compliance with applicable data protection laws. We take the findings of these assessments seriously and implement necessary measures to address any identified risks.

25. Third-Party Service Providers

We may engage third-party service providers to assist us in delivering our services. These providers may have access to your personal information as necessary to perform their functions, but they are prohibited from using your information for any other purpose. We ensure that all third-party service providers are compliant with applicable data protection laws and have appropriate safeguards in place to protect your information.

26. Social Media and Public Forums

Our website may include social media features and widgets, such as the Facebook Like button and Twitter share buttons. These features may collect your IP address, the page you are visiting on our site, and may set a cookie to enable the feature to function properly. Your interactions with these features are governed by the privacy policy of the company providing it. We encourage you to review the privacy policies of any social media platforms you engage with.

27. User Consent

In certain circumstances, we may rely on your consent to process your personal information. When we do so, we will provide you with clear information about the purpose of the processing and obtain your explicit consent before proceeding. You have the right to withdraw your consent at any time, and we will respect your decision and cease processing your information unless we have another lawful basis to do so.

28. Data Protection Compliance

We are committed to complying with all applicable data protection laws and regulations. This includes the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) for individuals in the European Union and other relevant laws in jurisdictions where we operate. We regularly review our policies and practices to ensure compliance and to protect the rights of individuals regarding their personal information.

29. User Feedback and Suggestions

We welcome your feedback and suggestions regarding our privacy practices. If you have any ideas on how we can improve our privacy policy or practices, please feel free to reach out to us. Your input is valuable to us as we strive to enhance our services and ensure the protection of your personal information.

30. Data Integrity and Accuracy

We take reasonable steps to ensure that the personal information we hold is accurate, complete, and up to date. We encourage you to review and update your information regularly to help us maintain its accuracy. If you believe that any information we hold about you is incorrect or incomplete, please contact us, and we will take steps to correct it.

31. Security Breach Response Plan

In the event of a security breach that compromises personal information, we have a response plan in place to address the situation promptly. This includes assessing the breach, notifying affected individuals, and taking steps to mitigate any potential harm. We will also review our security measures to prevent similar incidents in the future and ensure compliance with legal obligations regarding breach notifications.

32. Data Anonymization and Aggregation

We may anonymize or aggregate your personal information for research, analysis, and reporting purposes. Anonymization involves removing identifiable information so that you cannot be identified from the data. Aggregated data is combined with information from other users to create statistical insights. This type of data is not subject to this Privacy Policy and may be used for various purposes without restriction.

33. User Account Security

If you create an account with us, you are responsible for maintaining the confidentiality of your account credentials and for restricting access to your account. We recommend that you use strong passwords and change them regularly. If you suspect any unauthorized use of your account, please notify us immediately so we can take appropriate action to secure your account.

34. Data Protection by Design

We incorporate data protection principles into our business processes and systems from the outset. This includes conducting privacy impact assessments for new projects, implementing data protection measures during the design phase, and ensuring that privacy considerations are integrated into our operations. By adopting a proactive approach to data protection, we aim to minimize risks and enhance the security of personal information.

35. User Rights Under GDPR

If you are located in the European Union, you have specific rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). These rights include the right to access your personal data, the right to rectification, the right to erasure, the right to restrict processing, the right to data portability, and the right to object to processing. We are committed to upholding these rights and will respond to requests in accordance with applicable law.

36. Data Protection Training

We provide regular training to our employees on data protection and privacy best practices. This training covers topics such as data handling, security measures, and compliance with applicable laws. By educating our staff, we aim to foster a culture of privacy and ensure that everyone understands their responsibilities regarding personal information.

37. User Consent for Marketing

We will only send you marketing communications if you have opted in to receive them. You can opt-out at any time by following the unsubscribe instructions provided in our emails or by contacting us directly. We respect your preferences and will ensure that your choices are honored promptly.

38. Data Protection Impact Assessments

We conduct Data Protection Impact Assessments (DPIAs) for projects that may pose a high risk to individuals' privacy. DPIAs help us identify and mitigate potential risks before processing personal data. We take the findings of these assessments seriously and implement necessary measures to address any identified risks.

39. User Feedback and Suggestions

We welcome your feedback and suggestions regarding our privacy practices. If you have any ideas on how we can improve our privacy policy or practices, please feel free to reach out to us. Your input is valuable to us as we strive to enhance our services and ensure the protection of your personal information.

40. Conclusion

We are committed to protecting your privacy and ensuring the security of your personal information. This Privacy Policy outlines our practices regarding the collection, use, and protection of your data. We encourage you to review this policy regularly and contact us if you have any questions or concerns. Thank you for trusting Mossleies Care with your personal information.
